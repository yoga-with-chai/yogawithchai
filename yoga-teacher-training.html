A Glimpse into My 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training

Spending 3 weeks immersed in yoga at the Gayatri School felt like hitting pause on the outside world and tuning in fully to body, breath, and purpose. The days were long but deeply rewarding—filled with movement, learning, stillness, and connection. Whether we were on our mats flowing through asanas or sitting in silence at sunrise with a cup of tea, every moment was part of the journey. Here's what a typical day looked like during our 200-hour Hatha and Vinyasa training:

  • 06:30 – 07:30: Wake up, morning tea & silent practice (Antar Mouna)

  • 07:30 – 07:45: Meditation

  • 07:45 – 08:00: Pranayama (breathwork)

  • 08:00 – 09:00: Vinyasa or Hatha yoga class

  • 09:00 – 10:00: Breakfast

  • 10:00 – 11:30: Theory lesson (yoga philosophy, anatomy, etc.)

  • 11:30 – 13:00: Asana alignment or sequencing workshop

  • 13:00 – 15:00: Lunch break / Karma yoga / Self-study

  • 15:00 – 16:30: Afternoon theory lesson

  • 16:30 – 18:00: Asana alignment or sequencing workshop (continued)

  • 18:00 – 19:00: Evening Hatha or Vinyasa yoga class

  • 19:00 – 20:30: Dinner & Karma yoga

  • 20:30 – 22:00: Self-study & bedtime

Each day left me feeling a little stronger, a little clearer, and a lot more inspired !

I didn’t leave with all the answers, but I left with more confidence, a deeper connection to the practice, and a genuine desire to share it with others.
